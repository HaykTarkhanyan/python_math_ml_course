# 08 ML for Time Series — outline

Lecture 2 of the time-series chapter. 27 pages (25 frames; two predict-first
frames use `\pause`). Clean PDF only (not delivered yet). Builds on lecture 07's
"time-aware validation" hand-off.

## Sections / frames

1. **Cold open** — "where we left off"; reuse our regressors on a series by making
   it a table.
2. **Forecasting as supervised learning** — sliding-window → (X, y) design matrix.
   *(fig: supervised_reframe)*
3. **Leakage and time-aware splitting** — predict-first: random split = leakage;
   random vs forward split. *(fig: time_aware_split)*
4. **Feature engineering for time** — four families: lags, (shifted) rolling,
   calendar/Fourier, exogenous. Golden rule = knowable-at-t.
5. **Time-aware cross-validation** — `TimeSeriesSplit` forward-chaining. *(fig: ts_cv)*
6. **Tree models and the extrapolation trap** — predict-first: GBM flat-lines on a
   trend (trees can't exceed training max); fix = predict the difference; feature
   importance; recursive vs direct multi-step. *(figs: gbm_forecast,
   feature_importance)*
7. **So which wins?** — honest MASE comparison (seasonal-naive vs SARIMA vs GBM);
   when ML vs classical wins (M5 vs M3/M4). *(fig: model_comparison)*
8. **Deep learning and foundation models** — DeepAR/N-BEATS/TFT/PatchTST lineage;
   foundation models (TimesFM, Chronos-2, Moirai-2, TimeGPT, Time-MoE) with a
   "try the simple thing first" reality check. *(fig: deep_ts_timeline)*
9. **Recap** + toolbox list (sktime, statsforecast/mlforecast, skforecast, Darts,
   Prophet, GluonTS).
