In each of these experiments, there are exactly two possible outcomes. We

Definition
If a random variable X has exactly two possible values, say 0 and 1, we say
that X follows a Bernoulli distribution:
P[X = 1] = p,
P[X = 0] = 1 −p
and write X ∼Bernoulli(p).

Definition

P[X = k] = (1 −p)k−1p,
k = 1, 2, 3, . . .
we say that X follows a geometric distribution with parameter p, and write
X ∼Geo(p).

Why is this useful?

variables.

Poisson, etc.) and use the properties of those (widely known) distributions
to make predictions about future samples.
