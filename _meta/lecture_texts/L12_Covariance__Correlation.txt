Take X and Y
Subtract their means to see how much they deviate from the average
Multiply those deviations
(so that pos × pos = pos, neg × neg = pos, and pos × neg = neg)
Take the average of those products
E

(X −E[X]) · (Y −E[Y ])

This number is called the covariance between X and Y , and it measures
how much the changes in X and Y are related to each other.
