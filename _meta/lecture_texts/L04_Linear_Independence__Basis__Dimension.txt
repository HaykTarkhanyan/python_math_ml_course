--- Slide 33 ---
Linear Independence
Geometrically,
Two vectors are linearly dependent if they lie on the same line,
Three vectors are linearly dependent if they lie on the same plane,
and so on.
There is also another characterization of linear independence (try to prove
it by yourself):
Theorem
Vectors v1, . . . , vn are linearly independent if and only if the equation
c1v1 + c2v2 + . . . + cnvn = 0
is only true if c1 = c2 = . . . = cn = 0.
(i.e. if you plug in any numbers other than 0, the sum will not be 0).
