| Component | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|

---

## Weekly Mini-Quizzes

- 5 short questions at the start of each lecture (starting Week 2)
- Cover the previous week's material
- Mix of multiple choice, true/false, and one short calculation
- **Lowest 2 scores dropped** — life happens
- Purpose: keep students engaged week-to-week, not just before exams

### Sample quiz questions by week

**Week 2 quiz (covers Week 1):**
1. If P(A) = 0.3 and P(B) = 0.5 and A, B are independent, what is P(A and B)?
2. True or false: P(A|B) = P(B|A) always.
3. A box has 5 red and 3 blue balls. You draw 2 without replacement. What's P(both red)?

**Week 7 quiz (covers Week 6):**
1. Can the median and mean of a dataset be equal? When?
2. True or false: A dataset with no outliers always has a symmetric distribution.
3. What does Anscombe's quartet demonstrate?

## Homework Assignments

Three assignments, spaced evenly through the course. Each has ~5-8 problems, mix of calculation and conceptual questions.

### HW 1 — Probability & Distributions (due end of Week 5)
Covers: Weeks 1-4 (probability, random variables, expectation/variance, distributions)

**Sample problems:**
- Bayes' theorem applied to medical testing (false positive rates)
- Compute E[X] and Var(X) for a given distribution
- "Which distribution?" — match real-world scenarios to distributions
- Birthday problem variation

### HW 2 — Estimation & Confidence Intervals (due end of Week 9)
Covers: Weeks 6-9 (descriptive stats, estimation, MLE, CIs, bootstrap)

**Sample problems:**
- Given a dataset, compute descriptive statistics and identify outliers
- Derive the MLE for a simple distribution (e.g., Exponential)
- Interpret a confidence interval (correct vs incorrect interpretations)
- Explain the difference between SE and SD
- Prediction interval vs confidence interval

### HW 3 — Inference & Testing (due end of Week 12)
Covers: Weeks 10-12 (hypothesis testing, A/B testing, ANOVA)

**Sample problems:**
- Given a study, identify H0, H1, compute the test statistic, interpret the p-value
- "Is this p-hacking?" — identify methodological issues in a described study
- Power analysis: how many samples needed to detect a given effect?
- One-way ANOVA: given group means and variances, compute the F-statistic
- Design an A/B test for a given scenario

## No Midterm, No Final Exam

This is a practical course. The project replaces a final exam. The quizzes and homework provide continuous assessment.
